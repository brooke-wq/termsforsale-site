1. System Overview

How It Works

2. Deal Submission Flow

What Happens When a Partner Submits a Deal
  1. Form submitted at /submit-deal — partner enters contact info, property, deal type, numbers, photos, docs
  2. GHL Contact upserted — partner's contact created/updated with all custom fields (property address, deal type, prices, terms, photos, docs)
  3. Tags applied — dispo-buddy, jv-partner, jv-submitted, + deal type tags (db-cash, db-subto, etc.)
  4. Opportunity created — in "3. JV Deals" pipeline, stage "New JV Lead", with monetary value = asking price
  5. Notion page created — in deals database with status "New Submission" and all deal data mapped
  6. Confirmation SMS — sent to partner: "we got your deal, reviewing in 24-48hrs"
  7. Confirmation email — branded email with deal summary + next steps
  8. Internal SMS alert — sent to Brooke with deal summary
  9. Internal email alert — detailed email with all deal data + GHL link
  10. Contact note — full deal summary added to GHL contact notes
Safety: All SMS/email is gated behind NOTIFICATIONS_LIVE=true env var. CRM writes (contacts, opportunities, tags, Notion) always work regardless. Set to true only after testing.

3. Fee Split Structure

Tiered JV Split

The fee split is tiered based on partner performance. The JV agreement sent at "Ready to Market" reflects the appropriate tier for each partner.

TierSplit (Dispo Buddy / Partner)Criteria
Core 50 / 50 Default for all new partners
Proven 30 / 70 Partner has hit $25K in funded fees to Dispo Buddy OR closed 3 deals with us in a single quarter

How to track it: Use the contact tags db-proven-partner (once either threshold is hit) and keep the JV agreement template version matched to the tier when you send it out. Bump happens at the next deal submission — not retroactively.

Quarterly reset: The "3 deals in a quarter" criteria resets each calendar quarter. The $25K lifetime threshold does not reset.

4. JV Deals Pipeline — Stage Workflow

Each deal progresses through these stages in the "3. JV Deals" GHL pipeline:

StageWhat HappensWhoAction Required
New JV Lead Deal just submitted. Auto-created by the form. System Review within 24-48 hours
Missing Information Deal needs more info from partner (missing photos, docs, numbers) Team First fill in the opportunity "Missing Info" field with exactly what's needed (SMS/email uses this value), then move stage. Move back to Under Review when partner responds.
Under Review Team is reviewing the deal — checking comps, numbers, marketability Team Run underwriting, check ARV, verify contract. Decision: accept or reject.
Ready to Market Deal accepted. JV agreement sent to partner. Brooke Send JV agreement. Once signed, move to Actively Marketing.
Actively Marketing Deal is live on Terms For Sale. Buyers are being notified. System/Team Update Notion status. Monitor buyer interest. Follow up with leads.
Assignment Sent Buyer found. Assignment contract sent. Brooke Coordinate with title company. Confirm EMD.
Assigned with EMD Buyer has signed + EMD deposited. Brooke Monitor closing timeline. Keep partner updated.
Closed Deal closed. Fees disbursed by title. Brooke Confirm partner payment per JV agreement split. Update Notion. Celebrate.
Not Accepted Deal declined — bad numbers, no contract, or not viable. Team First fill in the contact rejected_deal_feedback custom field with specific feedback, then move to this stage. The rejection email auto-sends with that feedback embedded.
Automated notifications: Moving an opportunity between stages automatically fires SMS + email to the partner via GHL workflows. You don't need to manually notify them — just move the stage and the system handles the rest.

5. Step-by-Step: Processing a New Submission

When you get the internal alert SMS/email:
  1. Open GHL — Click the "View in GHL" link in the internal email, or search the contact name
  2. Review contact custom fields:
    • Property address, city, state, ZIP
    • Deal type (Cash, SubTo, Seller Finance, Hybrid, etc.)
    • Contract price, asking price, ARV, entry fee
    • SubTo details (balance, rate, payment) or SF details (amount, rate, term)
    • Photos link, documents link
    • Important details / notes from partner
  3. Check Notion — Find the deal in the deals database (status: "New Submission"). All fields should be populated.
  4. Quick viability check:
    • Is there a real spread? (Asking vs Contract vs ARV)
    • Does the partner have the property under contract?
    • Are photos and docs provided?
    • Is the market viable? (AZ, TX, FL, KY, TN, GA, OH, IN preferred)
  5. Decision:
    • Need more info → Move to "Missing Information" → Text partner
    • Not viable → Move to "Not Accepted" → Send rejection feedback
    • Viable → Move to "Under Review" → Run full underwriting

6. Processing Accepted Deals

After underwriting confirms the deal is good:
  1. Check tier — Does the partner have the db-proven-partner tag? If yes, use the 30/70 JV agreement template. Otherwise use 50/50.
  2. Move to "Ready to Market" in GHL pipeline — this triggers the auto-email + SMS with the JV agreement link
  3. GHL Documents sends the contract for e-signature automatically (or send via DocuSign if you prefer)
  4. Wait for signed agreement — you'll get notified in GHL when signed
  5. Update Notion — Change deal status from "New Submission" to "Actively Marketing"
  6. Move opportunity to "Actively Marketing" — triggers auto "Your deal is live" email + SMS to partner
  7. notify-buyers cron on the Droplet automatically matches the Notion deal to buyer criteria and fires SMS + email alerts to the Terms For Sale buyer network
  8. Monitor buyer interest — Track views, inquiries, and offers in GHL
  9. When buyer found → Send assignment contract → Move opportunity to "Assignment Sent" (auto-notifies partner)
  10. When EMD deposited → Move to "Assigned with EMD" (auto-notifies partner)
  11. When closed → Move to "Closed" → Confirm payout paid through title per JV agreement. Check if partner now qualifies for db-proven-partner tag.
Partner communication: Most partner notifications are automated via GHL workflows that fire on stage changes. The dashboard at /dashboard shows partners their deal status in real time.
Promoting to Proven Partner: After a deal closes, check the partner's cumulative funded fees and quarterly deal count. If they hit $25K total funded OR 3 closed deals this quarter, add the db-proven-partner tag. Their next deal gets the 30/70 JV agreement.
