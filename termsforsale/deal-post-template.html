Why This Deal Exists Right Now

The seller needed to move fast. Relocation, life change — the property sits vacant and they're done making payments. Rather than list and wait 60+ days, they agreed to sign over the deed and let a buyer take over their existing mortgage.

The result: you get a 2.75% fixed rate — locked in from 2022 — on a property in a zip code where rents have climbed 12% in the last 18 months. That rate alone saves you $400+/month versus buying at today's market rates.

💡 What is Subject-To?

You purchase the property and take over the seller's existing mortgage "subject to" the current loan terms. The deed transfers to you. The loan stays in the seller's name but you make the payments — and keep the rate, balance, and terms.
