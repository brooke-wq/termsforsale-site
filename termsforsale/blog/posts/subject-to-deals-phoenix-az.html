Subject-To Real Estate Deals in Phoenix, AZ — How to Lock In Below-Market Rates

By Deal Pros LLC  ·  Off-Market Deals  ·  Phoenix Metro

Phoenix is one of the most active sub-to markets in the country. Thousands of homeowners bought between 2019 and 2022 when 30-year rates were sitting at 2.5–3.5%. Today, with rates above 6.5%, those mortgages have become some of the most valuable assets in real estate — and motivated sellers are willing to transfer them.

Why Phoenix Sub-To Deals Work

The Phoenix metro — covering Scottsdale, Tempe, Chandler, Gilbert, Glendale, and Peoria — has one of the highest concentrations of 2020–2022 vintage mortgages in the Western US. Sellers in financial hardship, divorce, relocation, or simply cashing out equity have legitimate reasons to accept a subject-to structure rather than going through a traditional MLS sale.

For investors, the math is compelling. A $250,000 mortgage at 3% costs $1,054/mo in principal and interest. The same balance at 6.75% costs $1,622/mo — a difference of $568/mo, or $6,816/year, or over $200,000 over the life of the loan.

Phoenix Rental Market (2025)

Median rent for a 2bd/2ba in Phoenix: $1,450–1,650/mo depending on submarket. Vacancy rates in most Phoenix zip codes remain below 5%. Strong migration from California, Illinois, and the Northeast continues to drive rental demand.

Best Submarkets for Sub-To Investing in Phoenix

  • Northwest Phoenix (85023, 85027, 85053) — Airport proximity, Loop 101 access, strong renter demand from logistics and healthcare workers
  • Glendale / Peoria (85301–85345) — Affordable entry points, strong LTR demand, growing population
  • Chandler / Gilbert (85224–85296) — Higher ARVs but premium rents, tech worker demand from Intel, TSMC corridors
  • Mesa (85201–85215) — Dense rental market, proximity to ASU drives MTR demand
  • Tempe / Scottsdale corridor — Highest rents but highest entry, best for STR/MTR strategies

What to Look for in a Phoenix Sub-To Deal

  • Loan balance under 80% of current ARV — gives you equity cushion immediately
  • Original rate below 4% — the lower the rate, the bigger the cash flow advantage
  • PITI payment that leaves $200+/mo positive after management and vacancy
  • Vacant or seller-occupied — avoids tenant complications at close
  • HOA that permits rentals — always verify before submitting an offer
